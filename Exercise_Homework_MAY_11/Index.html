🏎️ The Formula 1

Formula One (F1) is the highest class of international racing for open-wheel, single-seater formula racing cars. It is a championship series sanctioned by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA) and is considered the pinnacle of motorsport. The term "formula" refers to the set of rules that all participating cars must adhere to.

The championships

Two major championships are contested: the Drivers' Championship (for the individual with the most points at the end of the season) and the Constructors' Championship (for the team with the most combined points).

Whats the F1 Sprint?

The F1 Sprint is a short race. It covers 100km – about one-third of a typical Grand Prix distance – and should last about 30 minutes. This length has been chosen to encourage a race that is dynamic rather than strategic, with no mandatory pit stop. While the bulk of the Formula 1 season consists of traditional Grand Prix weekends as we’ve covered above, six of them – a quarter of the 24 events in total – have been designated F1 Sprint events, which add another layer of excitement to proceedings. A Sprint weekend is still a three-day affair with the Grand Prix as the main event, but the Sprint Qualifying session (which sets the grid for the Sprint) replaces FP2 on Friday afternoon. The Sprint itself takes place on Saturday (instead of FP3), before Qualifying for the Grand Prix happens as normal.

🏁 General Facts

  • F1 stands for Formula One, governed by the FIA.
  • The first F1 race was in 1950 at Silverstone, UK.
  • Cars can hit over 370 km/h (230 mph).
  • F1 cars generate more downforce than their own weight.

👨‍✈️ Drivers & Teams

  • Michael Schumacher and Lewis Hamilton each have 7 world titles.
  • Ferrari is the most iconic and successful F1 team in history.
  • Max Verstappen is a current top driver with Red Bull Racing.

🔧 Technology & Strategy

  • F1 uses hybrid engines with electric energy recovery systems.
  • Strategy plays a big role: pit stops, tire wear, fuel, and weather.
  • DRS (Drag Reduction System) helps overtaking by reducing drag.

🌍 Global Impact

  • The F1 calendar spans 5 continents with over 20 races per year.
  • F1 aims to become carbon-neutral by 2030.
  • It's one of the most-watched sports in the world.

🧠 Fun Facts

  • Drivers can experience up to 6 G-forces during a race.
  • Each car can cost up to $15 million to build.
